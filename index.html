How to use this demo

The three views above are the three experiences inside Atrium. Use the switcher at the top of this page to move between them — they share the same fictional studio (Karkhana Architects, Mumbai) and the same seed data, so you can follow one studio across all three views.

Inside the Studio app you can also flip between two demo studios using the switcher in its sidebar — useful for showing how multi-tenancy works.

What the demo is and isn't

  • It's a high-fidelity clickable prototype — every screen looks and feels like the real product.
  • It's not connected to anything yet — no real AI calls, no real database, no real social scheduling. AI buttons cycle through pre-written variants so the demo feels alive.
  • Use it to validate the UX with friendly studios before committing to the real build.

Suggested order if you're showing this to a studio

  • Start in Overview for 30 seconds — frame the three pieces.
  • Open the Studio app. Show the dashboard, then click into House of Many Verandahs to show how AI generates four lengths of project narrative.
  • Go to the Outreach Kanban. This is the screen most small studios immediately want.
  • Open the Client view from a tab. Pitch this as the transparency wedge — clients see everything the studio is doing for their project.
  • Finish in Onboarding only if they ask "how does the AI know my voice?" — it's the answer to that question.
