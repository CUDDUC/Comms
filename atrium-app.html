Tagline · 12 words
A laterite house where every room remembers the verandah it once was.
Short blurb · 50 words · for Instagram and listing pages
Set in Siolim's old quarter, this 380 sqm house is organised around four verandahs that absorb light, monsoon, and family routine. Laterite walls, lime plaster and Burma teak frames carry the climate; corridors widen into rooms and rooms thin back into corridors. A house built more in section than in plan.
Mid feature · 150 words · for IndianArchitect&Builder, Stir World
The brief was modest: a holiday house for a family of five returning to a long-held plot near the Chapora. The site, narrow and east-west, asked to be read as a sequence rather than a footprint. We organised the plan as four verandahs of varying width — sometimes three metres, sometimes one — strung together by a single laterite spine wall that turns each time the section steps.

The rooms occur where these verandahs widen. None of the rooms have doors to the corridor — they share it, the way old Goan tile-roofed houses share their pavilions. Lime plaster catches the high south light through teak grilles, and the deepest verandah becomes a workshop for the family's three potters. The result is closer to a covered street than to a house — which, locally, is what a house used to be.

Brand voice — synthesised by AI, edited by you
v3 · 14 Apr
Trained on 6 published articles and your studio's about page.
Specific, not poetic
Karkhana writes with materials, dimensions and numbers in the sentence. Avoids "stunning", "seamless", "harmonious blend" — replaces them with the actual thing. A wall is not "elegant"; it is "375mm laterite, dry-stacked".
Section over plan
When describing a project, lead with the section idea — the vertical move, the layered space — and only then the plan. This applies to social, to award text, and to client conversations.
The studio behind the building
Always credit photographer, collaborators and craftspeople by name. Refers to ourselves as "we" not "the studio". Process is shown, not gestured at.
Do
  • Lead with the section or the material
  • Use rupees, sqm, mm — not "spacious"
  • Name the photographer in every post
  • Indian English, no full stops in headlines
Don't
  • "Iconic", "stunning", "blend", "elevate"
  • Open with "Nestled in..."
  • Use AI-feeling phrases ("in conclusion", "dive into")
  • Write in third person about ourselves
