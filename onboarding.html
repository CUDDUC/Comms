Step 8 of 9

Here's how we'll write for you.

Based on your three samples and your philosophy, here are three voice attributes and a do/don't list. Edit anything that doesn't sound right — every other AI feature in Atrium reads from this doc.

01
Specific, not poetic
You write with materials, dimensions and numbers in the sentence. Avoid "stunning", "seamless", "harmonious blend" — replace them with the actual thing. A wall is not "elegant"; it is "375mm laterite, dry-stacked".
02
Section over plan
When describing a project, lead with the section idea — the vertical move, the layered space — and only then the plan. This applies to social, to award text, and to client conversations.
03
The studio behind the building
Always credit the photographer, collaborators and craftspeople by name. Refers to the studio as "we" not "the studio". Process is shown, not gestured at.
Do
  • +Lead with the section or the material
  • +Use rupees, sqm, mm — not "spacious"
  • +Name the photographer in every post
  • +Indian English, no full stops in headlines
Don't
  • −"Iconic", "stunning", "blend", "elevate"
  • −Open with "Nestled in..."
  • −AI-feeling phrases ("dive into", "in conclusion")
  • −Third person about ourselves
